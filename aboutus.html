ABOUT US

Bapuji Rural Enlightenment and Development Society (BREDS) was established in 1983, with the goal to provide support to the most marginalized and vulnerable rural communities to elevate their socio-economic status through empowerment and sustainable livelihood promotion. BREDS firmly believes that building capacity in the communities is the only way to work through the poverty and create a significant change. BREDS underlying motive is to infuse the sense of ownership. Involving people at every level of decision-making within the programs to transform beneficiaries into participants, which will lead to inclusive development.


“Help the people to help themselves”



BREDS fundamental frameworks for programme interventions are based on Human- Centered methodology, which is solely based on participatory action research.

    •Understanding, observing and contextualization of the problem and the community
    •Community participation and brainstorming session with all the stakeholders to opt for best solution
    •Building capacity and ownership in the community to scale-up sustainability
    •Develop solutions rooted in communities’ needs and lifestyles for inclusive development.

OUR VISION

To realize the Gandhian dream of Gram Swaraj by building a network of autonomous village communities which are self-sufficient and self-reliant.

OUR MISSION

To capacitate the communities to become better informed and enlightened of their rights and choices in order to control the environment, resources and levels of power. To strength then the institutional base necessary for social transformation and to bring positive change in the quality of life of marginalized communities.

GOAL

To facilitate the process of empowerment of marginalized communities by reducing vulnerabilities and increasing capacities, and enabling them to liberate from oppression and exploitation and lead life with dignity and respect.

OBJECTIVES

-To infuse the sense of ownership to empower the community to increase the level of control over their lives and resources.
- To recognize and address the needs of the women for empowering and to promote gender equity.
-To protect and restore the local ecosystem for preserving natural resources.
- To provide easy and affordable financial solutions to low-income groups through promoting and strengthening Self Help Groups (SHGs) and Mutually Aided Cooperative Thrift Societies (MACTS).
-To promote agriculture as a viable livelihood option to improve food security and safety.
-To create resilient community to able to effectively respond, withstand, and recover from adverse situations and to reduce the harmful effects of all hazards.

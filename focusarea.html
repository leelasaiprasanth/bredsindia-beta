1.Urban poverty (with a focus of informal workers) and slum development

- The main focus is to reduce the insecurity and uncertainty of income and stabilize their livelihoods. 
-Slum development through access to basic infrastructure- access to clean water, sanitation, housings etc. 
- Access to benefits & entitlements to the informal workers under social security.
- Awareness and exercising workers rights.

Achievements:

- 3000 low skilled construction workers (1300 Female and 1700 Male) are organized under 5 societies to demand their rights and entitlements.
-400 women street vendors are registered under Street Vendors Act 2014.
-2 street vendors’ cooperatives have been promoted to provide affordable loans
-9 slum committees have been strengthened to ensure all basic amenities such as access to water, sanitation, power, land title deeds, functional aganwadi (pre-school), 100% coverage of anti- poverty schemes to all the inhabitants.
-Above 15,000 grievances have been addressed.
 

2.Gender-Based Violence and Enhancing Economic Empowerment of Women

-To encourage women actively participate in the community activities.
-Their needs are understood and ensure they are part of the project design.
  -Exercising women rights to prevent and Respond to Gender-based Violence.
-Promoting women ownerships to promote women empowerment.

Achievements:

-80 cases of gender-based violence have been identified and provided legal and counseling support. 

3.Financial Inclusion

-To encourage women to have access to financial resources.
-To promote different livelihood ventures .
-To inculcate the habit of thrift among women.
-To capacitate their skills to establish MACTS.

Achievements:

-18000 women are organized to form five cooperatives (2 in rural and 3 in urban area).
-20 women are employed in both cooperatives .

4.Sustainable Livelihoods & Food Security

-Comprehensive and integrated approach to create sustainable livelihoods.
-To control people migrating from rural areas to find better opportunities for livelihood, health, education for children.
- To work with the rural communities to make agriculture a viable livelihood option and fight poverty.
- To secure Food & Nutrition security in the vulnerable community (Children, Women, Pregnant & Lactating mothers) through ensurin access to social security schemes.

Achievements:

-113 villages including 1874 marginal farmer families have been benefitted.
-Each family will get a minimum of 1 lakh profit from the yields .
-Water and soil conservation activities are taken.
-Community asset base is created.
-Organic farming and horticulture .
-85 Toilets have been constructed  at Savarabejii village.
  -100% institution deliveries recorded .
-100% Immunization recorded .
-100% beneficiaries received THR (take Home ration- Supplementary nutrition) now under Anna Amrutha Hastham mothers and Lactates eating -at Anganwadi center itself.
  -school building inaugurated with all amenities (Electricity, Drinking water and Toilet)  at Kapubejji village (Vandrai – worth of Rs. 1000000).
-1 no. Anganwadi got sanctioned at Vandrai village (Worth of Rs. 5,00,000) with all amenities.

 

5.Child Development

- to run and monitor 1098 child helpline to address the issues of child abuse, child labor and violation of child rights, with a vision to achieve a child friendly society.
-to prevent child marriages, child labor and trafficking.
-to identify the child issues, counsel and refer to different Government institutions .
-to eliminate the child labor .
-to enroll the identified children in government schools.

.

Achievements:

-115 School Dropout Children are re-enrolled in formal schools and Hostels.
- 27 Begging and Child Labor Rescued with the help of Police and Labor Department and provided shelter and education with the help of Community Welfare Centre .
- 86 Child Marriages were stopped with the help of Child Marriage Prohibition Officer, Police and Community Welfare Centre.
- 7 Girls rescued from trafficking .
- 4 Child Sexual Abuse Case- Provided Counseling to child and parents.
-First Investigation Report (FIR) filed & Charge sheet against the employer- Rs 20000/- compensation provided to child form employer.

Resource Management, Environmental Protection & Climate Change

BREDS has undertaken Community Based Tank Management projects. The objective of the project is to increase the income level by improving agriculture livelihood support with focus on social and environment components and focused on Rain water conservation, Wasteland development, Uncultivable land brought in to cultivation, increased food security, soil & moisture conservation, preservation of ground water.
